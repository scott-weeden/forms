Central Bank Forms

Use the links below to access the Central Bank forms and agreements you will need. Service and Access Setup provides more details and guidance on the steps required to begin using a new service or to modify access to current services or applications. If you are unable to locate the form you seek, please refer to the Central Bank Services section of Contact for a comprehensive list of service and support contacts who can assist you.

Lending Central Forms

All Discount Window forms and agreements, including instructions for completion, are located in this section of Central Bank Forms. For more information, visit the Discount Window (Off-site) website or contact your Discount Window District Contact (Off-site) if you have questions. NOTE: All Discount Window loans must be fully secured. Please see the Collateral Information (Off-site) page of the Federal Reserve Discount Window/Payment System Risk website for additional information.

If your depository institution is a U.S. branch or agency of a foreign bank, please contact your Discount Window District Contact (Off-site) for additional Discount Window documentation requirements.

Required Agreements

Form Links
Letter of Agreement
The Form of OC 10 Letter of Agreement evidences an institution's acceptance of the terms and conditions in Operating Circular No. 10
 OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)
Authorizing Resolutions for Borrowers
The Authorizing Resolutions for Borrowers evidences an institution's authorization to borrow from and pledge assets to a Reserve Bank.
OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)
Official OC-10 Authorization List
The Official OC-10 Authorization List is a list of individuals, including their titles, who are authorized to borrow and/or pledge/withdraw collateral as specified in the institution's Authorizing Resolutions for Borrowers.
 OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)
Letter of Agreement to Correspondent Credit and Payment Agreement
If your institution does not have a Federal Reserve account, please select a correspondent to receive Discount Window advances and make payments on your institution's behalf.
 OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)
Certificate
The certificate will provide the Federal Reserve Bank all of the information needed to make an effective UCC-1 financing statement filing against the Borrower.
OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)
The Legal Opinion of Foreign Outside Counsel and the Legal Opinion of United States Outside Counsel relate to the enforceability of agreements and interests and should be completed by legal counsel of non-U.S. borrowers.
OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)

Please submit completed agreements to your Federal Reserve Bank (Off-site).

Other Agreements and Documents

Form Links
Form of Letter of Agreement to Correspondent Credit and Payment Agreement
This agreement allows an institution to designate a correspondent as a settlement point for discount window advances.

Note: This agreement is required for institutions that do not have a master account.
OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)
Form of Agreement for Third-Party Custodian to Hold Collateral
This agreement allows an institution to designate a third party custodian to provide collateral custody services in connection with the Discount Window.
 OC 10 Agreements (Off-site)
FR 2046: Selected Balance Sheet Items for Discount Window Borrowers
The Federal Reserve collects balance sheet data from Discount Window borrowers on the FR 2046 to monitor Discount Window borrowing. Borrowers report six data items: total securities, federal funds sold and resale agreements, total loans (gross), total assets, total deposits and federal funds purchased and repurchase agreements.
 Form (Off-site)

Please submit completed agreements to your Federal Reserve Bank (Off-site).

Federal Reserve Bank Reporting Central FedLine Access Authorization List

Step 2: Establish End User Authorization Contacts

Form Links
Federal Reserve Bank End User Authorization Contact (EUAC) Designation and Authorization Form for FedLine® Web-Based Services and Other Business Applications ("EUAC Form") to be used by Depository Institutions
This form gives the EUAC authority to designate authorized individuals (Subscribers) to access the Reporting Central application using the FedLine Web Solution. When we receive the completed End User Authorization Contact Form, a credential will be issued to that EUAC. The EUAC will use the FedLine credential to access the EUAC Center, which enables the EUAC to submit Subscriber requests, reset FedLine Subscriber passwords and access Subscriber reports and FedLine documentation once credentials have been issued to your organization's Subscribers.

Step 3: Set Up FedLine Subscribers

Form Links
Subscriber Requests
An organization's EUAC should submit a separate Subscriber request via the EUAC Center within FedLine Home for each individual who will be authorized to access the Reporting Central Services. The Subscriber request authorizes the Federal Reserve Banks to create a credential granting the Subscriber access to the Reporting Central Services indicated within the request.
NOTE: The EUAC will receive an acknowledgement email from the Federal Reserve confirming the receipt of the request.
Submit via the EUAC Center within FedLine Home

All organizations (with either an ABA#, a CIN# or Only an RSSD ID complete this form

Step 4: Set Up Report Access

Form Links
Federal Reserve Bank Subscriber Access Request Form for Reporting Central (Form RC-1)
This form gives the EUAC authority to designate authorized individuals (Subscribers) specific report series access using the Reporting Central application. You can request access for all reports for which your organization is responsible using one form per Subscriber per legal entity. However, you can submit a spreadsheet if a Subscriber needs to request access to submit reports for more than 10 legal entities. Please note that you will not be able to access all reports you select on the form until the reports have been made available within the Reporting Central application. Once you have been granted access to a specific report series, you can simply logon to the Reporting Central application to key in your data or report file uploading.
Form (PDF)
All organizations will use their RSSD ID to complete this form

Reserves Central—Reserve Account Administration Forms

Form Links
Federal Reserve Bank End User Authorization Contact (EUAC) Designation and Authorization Form for FedLine Web Services and Other Business Applications
This form gives the EUAC authority to designate authorized individuals (Subscribers) who should be issued credentials to enable them to transact business using the FedLine Web or FedLine Advantage® Solutions. When we receive the properly completed End User Authorization Contact Form and perform applicable due diligence, a credential will be issued to the EUAC. The EUAC will use the FedLine credential to access the EUAC Center, which enables the EUAC to submit Subscriber requests, to reset FedLine Subscriber passwords and access Subscriber reports and FedLine documentation.
NOTE: A separate form must be submitted for each individual that will be designated as an EUAC. An individual listed as an “Alternate EUAC” must also be separately designated as an EUAC. This form must be signed by an individual listed on your organization's Official Authorization List (OAL).

Subscriber Access Level Descriptions.
